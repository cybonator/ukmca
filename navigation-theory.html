What is Medical evacuation (MEDEVAC)?

A MEDEVAC is the transfer of sick/injured crew from ship to shore (often by helicopter or rescue craft).

Procedures:

  • Master communicates with Maritime Rescue Coordination Centre (MRCC) under GMDSS.
  • Provide medical report via Radio Medical Advice (MSN 1768 / MGN 331).
  • Prepare casualty with stretcher/basket.
  • Coordination through SAR authorities ensures safe evacuation.

Additional Information:

Medical evacuation, or MEDEVAC, is a critical procedure in maritime operations where a crew member requires urgent medical attention beyond what can be provided on board. The process involves coordination between the ship's master, maritime rescue authorities, and medical professionals on shore.

Timely communication and proper preparation are essential for a successful MEDEVAC operation. The use of helicopters for evacuation requires specific safety protocols to be followed, including securing the landing area and ensuring the casualty is properly prepared for transfer.
